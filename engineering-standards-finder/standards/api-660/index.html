Shell-and-Tube Heat Exchangers

Scope

Design, materials, fabrication, inspection and testing of shell-and-tube heat exchangers. Identical to ISO 16812.

Key requirements (10)

  • Shell-and-tube heat exchangers for hydrocarbon process service (also as ISO 16812).
  • Companion to TEMA — API 660 is the procurement / engineering supplement.
  • Min tube wall: 2.1 mm (0.083") for CS, 1.65 mm (0.065") for SS — 25 mm OD typical.
  • Tube-to-tubesheet joint: strength-welded + light-rolled OR full-strength expanded; expansion ≥ 2× tube wall thickness.
  • Shell side velocity: 0.6–1.5 m/s typical for liquid; tube side 1–3 m/s.
  • Shell ΔP allowable: typically ≤ 70 kPa; tube ΔP ≤ 70 kPa unless specified.
  • Min thickness allowance for fouling: typically 0.0001–0.0005 m²·K/W per side.
  • Hydrotest: per ASME VIII Div 1 (1.3 × MAWP).
  • Differential thermal expansion handled by floating head, U-tube, or expansion bellows.
  • Vibration analysis (TEMA / HEDH): natural frequency, fluid elastic instability, vortex shedding, acoustic resonance.
