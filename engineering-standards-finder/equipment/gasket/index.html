Flange gasket — spiral wound (B16.20), ring joint (B16.20), or non-metallic (B16.21).

Applicable Standards (3)

ASME — 3 standards

ASME B16.20 Metallic Gaskets for Pipe Flanges — Ring-Joint, Spiral-Wound, and Jacketed
Edition: 2023 Edition Region: USA, Global
Materials, dimensions, marking for spiral-wound, RTJ, kammprofile and jacketed gaskets.
Key requirements (6)
  • Metallic gaskets for pipe flanges — ring-joint, spiral-wound, jacketed.
  • Spiral-wound: alternating metal strip + filler (graphite, PTFE, ceramic).
  • Ring-joint (RTJ): octagonal (most common) or oval; soft iron, low-CS, SS, monel.
  • Spiral-wound dimensions: per ASME B16.5/B16.47 flange face dimensions.
  • Inner ring (CG/CGI): prevents inward buckling at high pressure.
  • Color coding: outer ring color = winding metal; stripe color = filler material.
ASME B16.21 Nonmetallic Flat Gaskets for Pipe Flanges
Edition: 2021 Edition Region: USA, Global
Dimensions and tolerances for non-metallic full-face and ring gaskets.
Key requirements (6)
  • Nonmetallic flat gaskets for pipe flanges.
  • Materials: rubber, PTFE, compressed non-asbestos fiber (CNAF), graphite.
  • Standard thickness: 1.6 mm (1/16"), 3.2 mm (1/8").
  • For Class 150 / 300 services typically; not for higher pressures.
  • Inside dimension matches pipe ID; outside dimension fits flange bolt circle.
  • Full face or ring (raised face) configurations.
ASME B16.5 Pipe Flanges and Flanged Fittings: NPS 1/2 through NPS 24
Edition: 2020 Edition Region: USA, Global
Flange dimensions, ratings, materials and tolerances for NPS 1/2–24, Class 150–2500.
Key requirements (8)
  • Pipe flanges & flanged fittings for NPS 1/2 to 24.
  • Pressure classes: 150, 300, 400, 600, 900, 1500, 2500.
  • Class 150 CS: 285 psig at 100 °F, 200 psig at 400 °F.
  • Class 300 CS: 740 psig at 100 °F, 555 psig at 700 °F.
  • Flange types: weld neck, slip-on, socket weld, threaded, lap joint, blind.
  • Facing types: raised face (RF), ring-type joint (RTJ), flat face (FF).
  • Bore matching: weld neck bore selected to match pipe schedule.
  • Hydrotest: per pressure class temperature/pressure rating chart.
