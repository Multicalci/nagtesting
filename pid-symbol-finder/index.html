What is a P&ID symbol?

A P&ID (Piping and Instrumentation Diagram) symbol is a standardized graphic that represents a piece of equipment, a valve, an instrument, or a line type on a process drawing, so that any engineer reading the diagram — regardless of company or country — understands what it means. This finder covers 368+ symbols across pumps, valves, heat exchangers, vessels and tanks, rotating equipment, mixers and agitators, piping specialties, instruments, interlock and logic elements, signal and process lines, valve actuators, and motors and drivers.

Symbols are drawn to two conventions: ANSI/ISA-5.1, the standard most commonly used for instrument and valve symbols and instrument tag abbreviations in North American plants, and ISO 10628 or common industry practice, typically used for major equipment items like vessels, exchangers, and rotating machinery. Every symbol card shows which convention it follows so you can stay consistent across a drawing set.

Frequently asked questions

What is a P&ID symbol used for?

A P&ID symbol represents equipment, valves, instruments, or line types on a Piping and Instrumentation Diagram, giving every engineer, operator, and contractor a shared visual language for how a process plant is built and controlled.

What's the difference between ANSI/ISA-5.1 and ISO 10628 symbols?

ANSI/ISA-5.1 is the standard most often used for instrument and valve symbols and instrument tag abbreviations, especially in North America. ISO 10628 (or common industry practice) is typically used for major equipment items such as vessels, heat exchangers, and rotating machinery. Many plants mix both within the same drawing set.

Can I download P&ID symbols as vector files?

Yes — every symbol on this page can be exported as a transparent PNG, a white-background JPG, or a vector SVG, ready to drop into Visio, AutoCAD, SmartPlant, or Excel.

How do I find a symbol quickly?

Type any part of the name, tag, or alias into the search box — it's a true substring search, so typing "cool" finds every air-cooled and water-cooled exchanger, and typing a tag like "PSV" or "FT" jumps straight to that instrument.

What do the instrument tag letters (like FT, PSV, LIC) mean?

Those are ISA-5.1 instrument identification letters — the first letter is the measured variable (F = flow, P = pressure, L = level, T = temperature) and the following letters describe the function (T = transmitter, I = indicator, C = controller, SV = safety valve). Pipe Schedule Reference & Sizing

ASME B36.10M — OD, wall thickness & ID by NPS/schedule, plus a quick wall-thickness recommendation from process conditions

OD is fixed per NPS regardless of schedule. STD = Standard, XS = Extra Strong, XXS = Double Extra Strong. Data per ASME B36.10M carbon/alloy steel dimensions — verify against the current code edition and your piping class before use in final design.

Uses the ASME B31.3 straight-pipe wall thickness equation t = PD / (2(SE + PY)) with Y = 0.4, plus corrosion allowance and a 12.5% mill under-tolerance, then picks the thinnest listed schedule that satisfies it. Allowable stress values are simplified from ASME B31.3 Table A-1 for quick estimation only — confirm final wall thickness and material allowable stress against the current code edition, and have the design reviewed by a qualified piping engineer.
